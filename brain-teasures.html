The answer is, with 1 weighing we can detect the bag with nakli coins.
Place all the bags in a sequence. Now take out 1 coin from the first bag, 2 coins from the second bag, 3 coins from the third bag.... and N coins from the Nth bag. Collect all of these coins and weigh them on the weighing machine. Expected weight in case none of the coins were nakli = N(N+1) * W
Since there were some forged coins amongst the coins placed on the weighing machine, and the weight of nakli coins is 1 gm more than the original weight, therefore actual weight of all the coins kept on the weighing machine would be greater than N(N+1) * W. The difference between the actual weight and the expected weight divided by the weight of coin gives the bag that contains nakli coins. For example,
Lets say there are 5 bags and weight of original coins is 10 gm each and the weight of nakli coins is 11 gm each. We place all the bags in sequence and pick out 1 coin from bag 1, 2 coins from bag 2, 3 coins from bag 3, 4 coins from bag 4, 5 coins from bag 5.
Expected weight considering none of the coins are nakli = (1+2+3+4+5)*10 = 15 * 10 = 150 gms
Now lets say the actual weight turns out to be 153 gms. Since the nakli coins were 1 gm heavier thann the original coins, it means that there are 3 nakli coins placed on the weighing machine. Now since 3 coins were taken out from bag 3, therefore bag 3 contains the nakli coins.
